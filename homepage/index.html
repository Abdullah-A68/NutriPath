What Is BMI and Why It Matters for Your Health

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a key indicator, a simple calculation of weight relative to height, categorizing you as underweight, normal, overweight, or obese according to WHO standards. Gender differences are crucial: women typically have about 10% more body fat than men at the same BMI, influencing calorie needs and health risks. While a screening tool, BMI helps identify potential health risks like diabetes, heart disease, and hypertension linked to weight.

Incorporating Physical Activity: Adjust Your Daily Calorie Needs

Physical activity significantly increases your daily energy expenditure. Our tool allows you to accurately select your activity levels, ranging from sedentary to very active, ensuring precise calculations.

We calculate your Estimated Energy Requirement (EER) based on your age, gender, BMI, and chosen activity level, adhering to guidelines from the USDA and NHS. For instance, a moderately active 30-year-old female with a normal BMI might need around 2,000 kcal/day, while a sedentary male of the same age could require less.
